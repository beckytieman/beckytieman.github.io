Bear Lake Fishing

by Jared Holmes

The frigid waters of Bear Lake are starting to warm up which means the fishing is about to get hot! Everyone knows that the cutthroat can be notoriously hard to catch, but we have a few tips to help even the newest angler come home with a big catch. Dont have a boat? Try an alternate flotation device like a kayak or even a simple float tube. Trolling will help you find the sweet spot, but if ypu are unable to troll or find yourself near the shore try, vertical jigging. When you do feel that nibble, be patient! Trout can be sneaky, but they're also persistent. Odds are if they tried once and you couldn't sink the hook, they'll be back for more. If you do have access to a boat or flotation device be mindful of the winds in the spring. As always, plan to dress warmly and proctect your self from the sun. We would love to hear your fishing stories this season, please mail in or email any bragging pictures to HCWeather@hcw.com and you might see your big catch right here on High County Weather.
